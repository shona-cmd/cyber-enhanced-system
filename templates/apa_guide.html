A Beginner’s Guide to the APA 6th ed. Referencing Style

1. In-Text Citations

Use (Author, Year) or Author (Year) in the narrative.

Parenthetical: (Smith, 2015)
Narrative: Smith (2015) found that…
  • Two authors: (Jones & Lee, 2018)
  • Three–five authors: First citation (Brown, Green, & White, 2019); later (Brown et al., 2019)
  • Six or more authors: (Garcia et al., 2020)
  • No author: (“Title of Article,” 2021)
  • No date: (Author, n.d.)

2. Reference List – Basic Formats

TypeFormatExample
Journal article (1 author)Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, volume(issue), page-range. DOI Smith, J. (2015). Effects of caffeine on memory. Journal of Cognitive Psychology, 27(4), 345–356. https://doi.org/10.1080/20445911.2015.1023456
BookAuthor, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher. Johnson, R. (2019). Understanding APA. Academic Press.
Edited book chapterAuthor, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. Editor (Ed.), Title of book (pp. xx–xx). Publisher. Lee, M. (2020). Citation rules. In P. Brown (Ed.), APA handbook (pp. 45–60). Sage.
Web pageAuthor, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL World Health Organization. (2021, March 15). COVID-19 guidelines. WHO. https://www.who.int/covid19

3. Common Rules

  • Reference list is alphabetical by first author’s surname.
  • Use hanging indent (0.5 in / 1.27 cm).
  • Only the first word of a title and subtitle (and proper nouns) are capitalised.
  • Include DOI when available; if none, give a stable URL.

4. Quick Checklist

Checkmark Author(s) listed exactly as they appear
Checkmark Year in parentheses after author
Checkmark Title italicised for books/journals
Checkmark Volume italicised, issue in parentheses (not italicised)
Checkmark DOI or URL at the end
